Direction C — Stacked composed-then-list (no toggle)
Both views always visible: compact lane × stage composition on top, flat member list below. No toggle, no mode state.
  • "Structure over scrolling" served by the always-visible composed strip.
  • Trade-off: double chrome cost; composed strip uses ~120px before the list starts.
  • Honest when the operator's task is "scan structure THEN drill into a member" — they don't toggle, they look up then down.
  • Risk if member count grows large: list scrolls past the composed strip and the lane × stage info is no longer in view.
  • Empty-members fallback: composed strip drops; surface degrades to the artifactPath-or-empty-state shape (same fallback as Direction B).
