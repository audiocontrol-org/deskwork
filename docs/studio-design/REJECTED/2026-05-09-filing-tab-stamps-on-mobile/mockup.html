PatternNative-mobile feel within press-check vocabulary. Two design choices that go together: (1) stamps shaped like filing-folder tabs because mobile readers swipe through stacked papers — the silhouette IS the metaphor; (2) a floating Compose chip in the bottom-right corner because that's where mobile readers' thumbs land for primary creation. Both choices step away from the desktop's "rubber-stamp-on-paper" idiom and step toward "I'm holding the calendar in my hand."

Stamp metaphorFiling-folder tab. Flat rectangle, paper-3 fill (slightly darker than the page so it reads as "a piece of paper that's been cut and stuck on top"), bottom-right corner cut at 45° via clip-path: polygon(0 0, 100% 0, 100% calc(100% - 7px), calc(100% - 7px) 100%, 0 100%), 3px stage-color rule on the left edge identifying the file. Text in JetBrains Mono with 0.16em tracking — engineered tab-label, not handwritten. The notched silhouette is what reads "this is filed under" — color stays restrained to a left-edge bar plus the tab content text. No rotation, no border, no off-kilter — but the geometry is unmistakable.

Creation actionsFloating Compose chip, bottom-right, 16px from edges, sitting above the tabbar. A flat red-pencil rectangle (NOT a round Material FAB — the round shape and elevation shadow would break the press-check vocabulary). Light inset highlight + warm-red drop shadow to give it just-enough lift without becoming Material. The chip carries the "+ Compose" mono caps label so it reads as a labeled affordance, not a glyph-only puzzle. Tap → the same Compose sheet from 1b. vs Compose-tab (1b): the FAB doesn't take a tab slot, which lets Folio return to the bar (tab 3 = Folio scrapbook overview, like the original picked Mockup 1). vs hero-strip-chip (1d): the FAB sits in the natural thumb-reach zone for primary actions, leaving the masthead clean.

StrongThe most-mobile-native of the three revised directions. The filing-tab silhouette and the floating compose chip both lean into iOS-Notes / Things-3 / native-mail conventions while staying inside the cream-paper / Newsreader / red-pencil palette. Compose is reachable from any tab without losing the third-tab Folio surface.

WeakTwo strong "this is mobile" cues at once. If the press-check aesthetic should stay close to the editor and review surfaces (which use straight rectangles for stamps, no clip-paths), the filing-tab silhouette risks feeling like a different design system. The floating chip overlapping content covers ~12% of the bottom-right corner of the body when scrolled past Compose-relevant content; this is a Material trade-off (FABs always cover content) and the operator should weigh whether that's acceptable for a primary creation affordance.
