PatternA hybrid: card-stream as primary, with a 52px dock for cross-cutting affordances. The dock is intentionally HALF the height of a tabbar — and it has three chips of different shapes (red + New idea, kraft-filled ▦ Press queue with badge, faded Done). That visual asymmetry signals "this is NOT a tabbar" — it's a strip of cross-cutting actions, which solves the entry-review continuity problem without conflating a list page with a tabbed page.

Why a dock and not a tabbarThe dashboard isn't navigated by switching tabs — it's read as a stream and acted-on per-card. A tabbar would force the operator to context-switch between list-of-things and queue-of-channels, a peer relationship that doesn't actually exist (the press queue is data ABOUT certain entries, not a peer surface to the entry list). The dock surfaces it as a chip — same one-tap reach as a tab, but visually accurate ("this is something on the side, not a sibling page").

+ New ideaRed-pencil chip in the dock surfaces an action the operator does often but couldn't easily reach in #2 (which had no chrome at the bottom). One-tap "/deskwork:add" — clipboard-copy of the slash command, per THESIS Consequence 2. The skill does the work; the dock just routes intent.

Done chipReserves the slot for #242's cross-cutting Cancel/Done affordance (Phase 4 work). On the dashboard the Done chip is a no-op (operator dismisses to ... what, the studio index?), but the slot exists so the visual rhythm of "always a Done chip on the right" carries across surfaces. Phase 4 will wire it.

Press queue sheetPer-channel chips (LinkedIn / Reddit / YouTube / Instagram), posted green and todo red, mirroring the existing distribution-matrix vocabulary. The sheet is 60% — same as the review surface's notes sheet — for muscle-memory reasons. Open a card from the sheet → goes to the entry's review surface where the operator runs /deskwork:distribute per channel.

StrongBest of both. The card-stream's directness; the entry-review's discoverable affordances at the bottom. The dock's asymmetry honestly represents the dashboard's nature: the page IS the list, plus a few cross-cutting verbs. Press queue stays one tap away without taking 33% of the chrome budget. Cancel/Done slot is forward-compatible with #242 without needing a re-design.

WeakTwo chrome-bottom patterns to remember (entry-review's 64px tabbar vs. dashboard's 52px dock). The 12px height delta is intentional but adds two CSS surfaces. The "+ New idea" chip's exact placement might want operator opinion — left-anchored is reachable; alternatives (right-anchored, dock-center) trade reach for visual balance with the Press chip. The dock requires a second mental model (chip-strip vs. tabbar) which is a small cognitive cost.
