Cross-cutting bar mockup · direction 1 · v0.21 prerequisite A 44px fixed bottom nav strip appears on every studio surface, carrying three top-level destinations: Desk · Shortform · Manual. The currently-active surface gets a red-pencil underline rule on its tab. Above the nav strip, each surface stacks whatever action affordance it needs — the dashboard floats a Compose pill at right, review surfaces lay 3 colored pills as a sticky strip, longform entry-review lays 4 chip-tabs (Outline · Notes · Scrapbook · Actions). The two regions are visually distinct: the action area uses paper-2 or transparent floats; the nav strip uses paper-3 with a kraft top-rule. Cost: 96px bottom chrome on surfaces with both regions.

The third draft retires state surfacing entirely on mobile.

Studio mobile-first — PRD.

Extend the press-check vocabulary across every studio surface.

Problem

The dashboard, shortform desk, and review surfaces all ship desktop-shaped CSS. On phone the strip flex layout breaks, controls scroll out of sight, and the operator can't review on iOS without scrolling back to the top each time.3

The v0.17 + v0.18 entry-review rebuild proved that press-check on mobile reads beautifully when the chrome is right.5

Scope

Four sequenced cuts: Dashboard → Shortform → Standalone scrapbook → Help + Index.

Action region: 4 chip-tabs (Outline · Notes · Scrapbook · Actions).
Nav strip: Desk highlighted (operator came from desk).

Operators get one anchor for "go to another surface" and one anchor for "act on this surface" — never the same anchor doing both jobs.
