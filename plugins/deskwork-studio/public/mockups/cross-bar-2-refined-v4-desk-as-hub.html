§ 5 v1 → v4 · the simplification arc

Four passes through the same problem, simpler each time.

v1 · 3-cell nav

Bar carries Desk · Short · Manual. Each surface highlights its tab. Contextual cap = 3. Scrapbook compresses to Actions sheet on entry-review.

v2-A · 2-cell nav

Manual moves to masthead ?. Bar has Desk · Short. Contextual cap = 4. Entry-review keeps Scrapbook in bar; TOC only fits on shortform.

v3 · 1 picker cell

Single picker cell shows current destination + ▾ chevron. Tap opens sheet listing destinations. Contextual cap = 5. TOC fits on both review surfaces. Two-tap destination switch.

v4 · No nav · Desk is hub

Bar has zero nav region; only "← Desk" link in masthead. Desk absorbs every reachable destination as a section/tile. Contextual cap = 6. One mental model. Two-tap destination switch (← Desk → tap target). Desk page grows.

The simplification
  • v1 → v2: "Manual doesn't deserve 1/3 of nav."
  • v2 → v3: "Why enumerate at all when we can collapse to one picker?"
  • v3 → v4: "Why have a picker when Desk can be the hub and one back-link suffices?"
Each step trades surface-chrome for hub-page complexity, and gains a clearer mental model. v4 is the asymptote — there's nothing left to remove from the bar's nav region (it's gone entirely).

§ 6 What v4 commits to · what it costs

Commitments (positive)
  • Single mental model: ← Desk returns home from anywhere. From Desk, drill into anything.
  • The bar's job is unambiguous: surface contextual work for the current surface.
  • Contextual cap grows to 6 cells. Entry-review longform's 4 v0.18 tabs + TOC + (future) other contextual fits with headroom.
  • Destination namespace lives in the Desk's information architecture, not in chrome — making it the operator's HOME in a real sense.
  • Surfaces with no contextual work (Manual) drop the bar entirely — the chrome budget honestly matches the work.
Costs (real)
  • Every destination switch is 2 taps. Tap ← Desk → tap target. Operator can't switch destinations in 1 tap like v1/v2-A allowed.
  • The Desk page grows. Longform pipeline tiles + Shortform section + Files tile + Folio tile + future tiles. Scroll cost increases on a single-screen view of Desk. Mitigated by the structure-over-scrolling principle (sections collapse) but not eliminated.
  • The Shortform desk surface may need to retire. If Desk shows shortform workflows by platform, /dev/editorial-review-shortform is duplicated chrome. Decide: retire the route, or keep it as a deep-link that mirrors the Desk section. Either way it stops being a peer destination.
  • The "Manual" surface may need a "Search" surface alongside it eventually. Without a global search anywhere in chrome, finding things deep in Manual or content tree requires the operator to navigate first. (Optional refinement: add a search affordance to the masthead alongside ?.)
Alternative worth flagging: "← Desk" in the bar instead of the masthead This mockup places the back-link in the masthead's upper-right (matching existing er-strip-back precedent). An alternative: put it in the bar's leftmost cell instead. The bar grows by 1 cell (back-link cell), contextual region drops by 1 (cap = 5 instead of 6). Tap target moves to the bottom of the screen for better thumb reach. Worth picking if the operator finds the masthead tap-target too high. Same architecture; different chrome.
