§ 4 The ⋮ scope convention

The vertical ellipsis is now an overflow affordance with two scopes, parsed by location:

⋮ at row level (dashboard rows — v0.20) Tap reveals that row's options: iterate · approve · cancel · scrapbook · induct · block (stage-aware). Source: cross-bar 2 v0.20 row-affordance design (shipped). The dashboard's per-row overflow uses this glyph.
⋮ at masthead level (this work — v6) Tap reveals studio-wide cross-cutting options: Manual · Keyboard shortcuts · Configure · File an issue · About. Same overflow vocabulary, different scope. Operators parse the scope by location — masthead vs row — and the affordance reads as "more options at this level."
Why this doesn't conflate The two ⋮ glyphs live in physically distinct chrome regions: the row-overflow is inside a row's foreground; the masthead-overflow is at the top of the screen. Operators wouldn't accidentally tap one when wanting the other — the spatial separation is the disambiguation. The pattern reads as "⋮ means 'more options for the thing you're touching it on.'"
