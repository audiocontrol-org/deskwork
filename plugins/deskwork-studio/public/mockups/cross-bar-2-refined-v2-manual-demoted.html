Option B trade-offs
  • Pro: Manual stays in the bar — operators don't have to learn the masthead ? pattern. The ? glyph still demotes visual weight (no label, smaller weight, faded color at rest).
  • Pro: Bar geometry stays at 3-cell nav, consistent with v1 refinement — no rewire of contextual region math.
  • Con: Doesn't recover the 4th contextual slot. Entry-review review-mode still compresses Scrapbook into Actions sheet (the v1 friction point survives).
  • Con: Mixing two visual weights for nav cells (Desk/Short with italic glyph + label vs. Manual with quiet ?) reads as "two tab types" rather than "three nav tabs," which is the same conceptual cost as Option A's masthead-? without the contextual-slot payoff.
Recommendation: if you want Manual to stay in the bar, Option B is the cleanest form. But if the operator's framing — "won't be used very often" — generalizes, Option A's full demotion captures the principle more honestly.

§ 5 What 2-cell nav unlocks

Other downstream payoffs from the 4-cell contextual max.

1 · Shortform review can carry TOC when the body has headings Today's shortform-3 mockup proposed a 3-tab bar (Actions / Versions / TOC). With 4 contextual cells available, Shortform can carry 3 verb cells (Iter / Apprv / Canc) plus a TOC tab in the 4th slot — conditional on tocEntries.length ≥ 2. Operator gets one-tap to act AND one-tap to navigate sections. Closes #244 inline. The 6-cell upper bound stays.
2 · Entry-review review-mode keeps Scrapbook at-a-glance The folio kraft badge on the Scrapbook tab surfaces the asset count immediately. Operators don't have to open the Actions sheet to learn how rich the entry's scrapbook is. Restores v0.18's shipped affordance that v1 refinement compressed away.
3 · Headroom for future contextual cells Surfaces that don't yet use the 4th slot (Dashboard, Shortform desk, Content tree, Scrapbook viewer, Studio index) have a contextual cell available if Phase 3+ design surfaces a need. The pattern's escape hatch ("More" tab → secondary verbs sheet) is still available beyond the 4th slot if a surface ever needs more than 4 — but no surface today needs it.
