PatternNo bottom-tab-bar at all. The dashboard's job is to surface a list of editorial units and let the operator act on them. A tab bar that swaps slices of THE SAME underlying list adds chrome that earns no signal — the operator can already see the list. So this direction commits to one continuous magazine-style stream: needs-attention first → in-flight → final → shipped → blocked. Each card carries its own stage information (kicker glyph + label + relative time), so the operator never asks "what stage is this in" — the kicker tells them in 1.5 line-heights.

Order is the affordance"Needs your attention" comes first because that's the work-shape: the operator opens the dashboard to find what they owe a decision on, not to admire the shape of the pipeline. In flight is everything else still moving through stages. Shipped and Blocked are recoverable side-tracks. The stream-divider "Needs your attention. · 3" reads like a magazine standfirst — Newsreader italic, count-as-meta, red rule under it.

Press queue interleavedThe desktop's sidebar press-queue isn't a peer surface here — it's a kind of card. Press-queue cards interleave into the stream where they belong (next to the entry whose channels are queued), with a kraft-toned background and "▦ Press queue · LinkedIn" kicker so the operator knows which kind of card they're looking at. The Channel chips ("LinkedIn" / "Reddit · posted") render the per-channel state without leaving the home page.

#237 / #238 / #243 fixes56px masthead + 40px filter strip = 96px chrome. Cards are 1-column, all interior text wraps via overflow-wrap: anywhere; no auto minmax(0, 1fr) auto auto grid. Pre-review cards simply omit the stamp — no em-dash placeholder.

StrongDirect manipulation. The operator's eyes hit the dashboard, find the three cards under "Needs your attention", tap the first one. No tab-swap, no scrolled stage section. The stream order encodes priority directly. The full bottom of the screen is content — no chrome to dodge with the thumb.

WeakLess continuity with the entry-review surface — the operator's muscle memory expects a bottom tab bar at the dashboard level too. Filter-by-stage now requires the chip strip to do work that tab swap did before; if the chip strip becomes the primary stage filter, it deserves a multi-select mode (currently single-select) which adds complexity. Card height on long titles can push 4-card screens to 3 cards — the density of the existing pipeline-row layout is harder to match.
