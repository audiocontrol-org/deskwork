PatternAuthoritative print-shop. Two design choices that go together: (1) stamps reduced to italic display text bracketed by hairline rules — the lightest possible "stamped" gesture, the way a print compositor would mark a proof; (2) creation chip lives in the masthead because that's where the publication says "I am" — creating a new entry IS a publishing act, not a peer of pipeline navigation. The aesthetic is editorial / quiet / typographic.

Stamp metaphorRule-bracketed mark. No box, no fill — just italic Newsreader display text in the stage color, bracketed by 1px top + 1px bottom rules in the same color. In review reads as a typeset section-mark in red-pencil, not a stamp at all. The rules carry the "marked" semantic; the italic display carries the "editorial" voice. Smaller stamps work better because the rules contain the eye even at 0.78rem. Vs letterpress-tag (1b): less geometric weight, more typographic. Vs filing-tab (1c): no shape ornament — pure typography.

Creation actions"+ New" chip in the masthead right slot, where Help used to live. The chip echoes the wordmark's box-and-inset-paper treatment exactly — same 2px red border, same inner paper bleed, same italic display + tracked mono "+" — so it reads as a sibling element of the masthead, not an intruder. Help relocates to the Compose sheet's footer (low-frequency action, off-the-bottom), keeping the strip clean. Vs Compose-tab (1b): no tab slot consumed, so Folio stays in the bar (3 original tabs). Vs FAB (1c): no chrome floats over content — every affordance has a home in the page chrome, not over the body. Cleaner reading surface.

StrongThe most typographically refined of the three revised directions. Stamps stop competing with row content — the rules let the eye scan the slug and title without a heavy color block in the way. Compose chip in the masthead frames creation as an editorial act rather than a peer-of-pipeline-nav. Nothing floats over content; nothing rotates. The whole surface reads as a typeset proof.

WeakStamps may read as TOO subtle for some operators — especially in a quick at-a-glance scan, the rule-bracketed mark needs the eye to land on it before the color registers (vs. the heavier letterpress-tag that announces itself). Hero-strip "+ New" is upper-left-quadrant on the screen — slightly less thumb-reachable than a bottom-right FAB. Help moves into the Compose sheet, which means operators have to know it lives there (current behavior puts it in the strip; this is a discoverability cost during the transition).
