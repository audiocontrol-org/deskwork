Studio mobile-first — PRD.

Extend the press-check vocabulary across every studio surface.

Problem

The dashboard, shortform desk, and review surfaces all ship desktop-shaped CSS. On phone the strip flex layout breaks, controls scroll out of sight.3

The v0.17 + v0.18 entry-review rebuild proved that press-check on mobile reads beautifully when the chrome is right.5

Scope

Four sequenced cuts: Dashboard → Shortform → Standalone scrapbook → Help + Index.

Entry-review (longform) /dev/editorial-review/entry/<uuid> v0.18 ships · unchanged
Idiom: 4-tab mobile-bar with sheet host.
Per-surface; no global nav. Back-link in masthead.
Three surfaces. Three idioms. One vocabulary glues them together — and the vocabulary is what's actually consistent. The chrome shapes match each surface's work.

The palette is the constant

--er-paper (cream page)
--er-paper-2 (raised surface)
--er-paper-3 (hairline)
--er-red-pencil (primary accent · masthead rules, section heads)
--er-proof-blue (links, iterate, secondary)
--er-stamp-green (approve, save success)
--er-kraft (scrapbook, folio surfaces)

Every studio surface reads from these tokens. Identical across dashboard / review / shortform / scrapbook / manual.

The typography is the constant

Newsreader italic — masthead titles, section heads, stage glyphs
JetBrains Mono — kickers, labels, slugs, meta
No Inter. No system-default sans. No Roboto.

The optical-size aware Newsreader means every italic glyph at every scale reads as the same family. Mono caps with 0.18em tracking carries every kicker. The two-family pairing is the wordmark of the studio.

The texture is the constant

A paper-noise SVG turbulence filter sits behind every raised surface — masthead, mobile-bar, FAB, sheets, stage tiles. Subtle (0.06 alpha) but always present.

background-image: var(--noise);

You feel it before you see it. It's what makes the studio feel like a workshop instead of a web app.

The folio is the nav

Every surface carries a folio masthead at the top — kicker (mono, red), title (italic display), meta (mono, faded). On non-home surfaces, a back-link sits at the right edge (proof-blue, mono, underlined). The folio IS the navigation chrome — operators have learned it across every surface that's shipped.

Direction 3's claim: this is sufficient nav. Adding a bottom-bar nav adds a second nav affordance, not a better one.

What Direction 3 means for v0.21 specifically

Picking Direction 3 means: the shortform mobile-first pass produces a shortform-shaped chrome (Shortform-1, 2, or 3 from the existing pick). The dashboard's compose-chip stays as it is. The entry-review longform 4-tab bar stays as it is. Scrapbook viewer (Phase 3) will design its own. Manual (Phase 4) will design its own.

The downstream commitment: each future surface's mockup pass invokes /frontend-design with a brief that says "design from the press-check vocabulary; don't assume the shape of any other surface's chrome." The cross-cutting consistency is the vocabulary, not the layout.

The implicit operator burden: learn each surface's chrome. The argument that this is OK: today's two surfaces (dashboard + entry-review) already differ, and operators have not complained. The friction the operator is naming on shortform is "decisions out of reach" — solvable by giving shortform good chrome, not by giving all surfaces the same chrome.
