B · folio peek tapped · sheet slides up · same press-check item grid as Mockup 1's sheet · kraft top-border (2px) signals continuity from peek

PatternThe folio peek is its own visual artifact — a kraft-warm strip wedged between the top chrome and the article body, ~36px tall, with a notched left edge cut like a hanging-file folder tab. The italic "Folio" label sits in the tab; the item count, chip thumbs, and viewer link fill the rest. Reads as press-check editorial native: the article is "in" a folio that has supporting materials, and the folder is peeking out from behind it.

Visual differentiationThis is the only mockup where the scrapbook is a different kind of thing visually, not just another peer in the existing chrome. The kraft tone (a warm desaturated tan, distinct from cream paper and paper-3 buff) gives it its own material identity. The chip thumbs preview format diversity at a glance — image, text, audio chips render with their format-specific gradient, so the operator sees "this entry has photos AND text AND audio" without opening anything.

Sheet contentSame press-check 2-column item grid as Mockup 1. Sheet head reads "Folio · 5 items" with the standalone-viewer link. The 2px kraft top-border on the sheet ties the visual chain back to the peek strip — the sheet IS the folio expanded.

Bar stays 3 tabsOutline / Notes / Actions unchanged. The original "thumb-reach for decisions" framing of Mockup 1 stays intact. Scrapbook isn't an action — it's context, and lives in the chrome above the article where context belongs.

StrongMost distinctive of the three directions. Press-check vocabulary expressed with a new visual element instead of stretching the existing one. The chip preview gives at-a-glance format diversity that no tab-counter can. The metaphor (article inside a folio with materials) is structurally true and reads instantly.

WeakAdds 36px of chrome between strip and article — non-trivial real estate at phone widths. The notch + clip-path needs careful CSS to not break on scroll-into-view. Operators on entries with zero scrapbook items see an empty peek strip permanently — needs a tasteful empty state (or display:none when count is 0).
