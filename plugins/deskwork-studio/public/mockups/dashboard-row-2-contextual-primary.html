PatternOne contextual primary verb per row. The row layout has three columns: row-body (slug + title + meta strip), primary verb chip (contextual to state), and overflow ⋮ (secondaries). The primary verb is what the operator most-likely-wants-to-do RIGHT NOW based on the entry's state: approve when reviewState is in-review, iterate when iterating, open for everything else (no state, approved, etc.). The ⋮ holds the longer-tail secondaries (cancel, scrapbook, induct).

Row height (default state)~70px regardless of state. Slightly taller than Direction A (the row-head meta strip costs a line) but stable. The row-head consolidates №+stamp+iter into one inline cluster instead of stacking them — that's the geometric trick that keeps the row stable.

Trade-offThe primary verb takes 80-90px of horizontal real estate from the slug/title body. On long slugs (e.g. deskwork-plugin/open-issue-tranche-proposal at 39 chars) that compresses the slug column to ~180px, forcing word-wrap. The row body still wraps gracefully (overflow-wrap: anywhere) but it's denser. Approve / iterate / open all become 1-tap from the dashboard. Cancel / scrapbook / induct are 2-tap (via ⋮ → sheet).

When it winsOperator's most-common-action stays at 1 tap. The dashboard becomes a triage queue: see what needs review → approve. The visible verb is also a HINT about what's expected of the operator (the in-review badge says "needs your attention" — the approve button says "specifically, your APPROVAL or rejection"). Reduces the cognitive load of "what should I do here?" because the row tells you.

When it doesn'tOperator wants to cancel several entries quickly — each cancel is 2 taps + sheet dismissal. Direction C (swipe) is faster for that. Also: the contextual primary's color (green for approve, blue for iterate) competes for visual weight with the stamp's color rail — at-a-glance the row has TWO color signals saying "here's the state" which is redundant. Could mitigate by making the chip flat (no fill), keeping color in the icon only.
