B · Drafting expanded inline · the tile rotates the chevron and turns red · other tiles stay reachable above + below · scroll cost = the tile's row in the list, not 750px

PatternEvery stage is a 36px tile in a stack of 8. The whole calendar fits above the fold (8 × 36 = 288px, plus the 96px chrome = 384px). The operator opens the dashboard and immediately sees the SHAPE of their pipeline — what's where, what's empty, what needs attention — without scrolling. Tap any tile (count > 0) → that stage's rows expand inline; the tile's chevron rotates, the tile turns red. Other tiles stay collapsed and reachable above + below the expanded section. Empty stages render the same shape but muted; their chevron is paper-3 (visually disabled). The currently-active filter chip's stage auto-expands so an operator who taps "Drafting" in the chip strip lands on the expanded Drafting view directly.

Scroll cost (default state)~96px to first stage tile. All 8 stages are visible at the top of the body; the operator scrolls 0px to see the entire calendar shape. Compare current implementation: 746px to first content row. With one stage expanded, the rows for that stage appear at ~96 + (n × 36) where n = the position of the expanded stage (e.g. Drafting = 4, so ~96 + 144 = 240px to its first row).

Trade-offThe ROW content is one tap away. The operator has to expand a stage before they see slugs, titles, and decision affordances. That's a tap they didn't have to make before — but they SAVED scrolling 750px first. For an operator who knows which stage they're looking at (and per the data, they almost always do), the tap is cheaper than the scroll. For an operator who wants to scan ALL rows in ALL stages, this pattern adds friction (they'd need to expand each stage one by one). The Active filter chip remains a fast path to "every entry that needs attention regardless of stage" for that scan.

When it winsThe operator opens the dashboard, sees "3 in review in Drafting · 1 approved in Final" at-a-glance, taps Drafting, sees the 3 entries needing decisions, taps an entry → review surface. Total: 2 taps, 0 scrolls. That's the by-far common path on this surface.

When it doesn'tThe operator wants to scan every entry's slug+title across the whole calendar (e.g., looking for an entry whose slug they half-remember). They'd have to expand all 4 non-empty stages and scroll. The Find search box at top can already do this; this pattern leans into the search box for that case.
