B · compact density · single-line rows (~32px each) · all 11 entries fit above the fold · stamp shrinks to 4-letter abbreviations · stage glyph on left rail to maintain stage cue without section heads being heavy

PatternTwo compactness moves stacked. First, empty stages don't render their full section head — they collapse into one thin "0 in Ideas · Outlining · Blocked · Cancelled · show ↓" footer at the bottom. Tap "show ↓" to expand them back. Second, the filter strip gains a density toggle (⊟ Compact / ☰ Standard). Standard is the current 3-line stacked row; Compact is single-line: stage-glyph + slug + stamp-or-blank + → arrow. Both moves can be active at once (frame B).

Scroll cost (default state)~96px to first content row in standard density (Planned head + first row, no preamble). With compact density, all 11 calendar entries fit in roughly one viewport (each row ~32px × 11 = ~352px + 4 stage heads × 36px = ~496px, plus 96px chrome = ~592px — easily within 844). The empty-stages footer is one line at the bottom.

Trade-offCompact density loses the title and action chips per row. The operator sees slug + stamp + arrow; to act, they tap → and land on the entry's review surface where actions live. The "open / approve / cancel / scrapbook" inline chips disappear. That's a one-tap-deeper cost for the actions per entry — but the alternative (3-line standard rows) doesn't fit a meaningful number of entries on phone anyway. Standard density preserves the inline actions but only fits 2-3 rows at a time. The toggle lets the operator pick: SCAN mode (compact) vs ACT mode (standard).

When it winsOperator opens the dashboard, glances at the compact list, sees "I have 3 in review in Drafting and 1 approved in Final" in one viewport. Taps the in-review entry that's most pressing → review surface. Returns to dashboard, taps the next. Browse-and-act loop is fast because they never lose context to scrolling. Power users will likely set Compact as their default and never see Standard.

When it doesn'tAn operator who wants to act-from-the-dashboard (approve/iterate/cancel without going to the review surface) loses inline actions in compact mode. Standard mode preserves them but at the scroll cost. The density toggle is a manual choice the operator has to remember exists; some operators will never find it. For a NEW operator, the standard 3-line layout is more discoverable — the operator can see what each row's parts ARE without first learning that Compact exists.
