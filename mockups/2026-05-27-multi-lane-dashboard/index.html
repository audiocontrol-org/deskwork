Multi-lane dashboard — three structural directions

Phase 4 made the verbs template-aware. Phase 5 surfaces multiple lanes in the studio. These three mockups resolve the same problem differently within the established press-check aesthetic.

The decision

How should N lanes be surfaced in one viewport?

The existing Desk surface is a stack of sections. With multi-lane, the Longform domain now has multiple parallel pipelines, each with its own stage list. The structural question: do lanes stack vertically (more sections), switch laterally (sub-navigation), or co-exist as horizontal swimlanes (kanban-of-kanbans)?

Each direction picks one answer. Tap through to see fixture data: 3 lanes (Editorial 6-stage, Mockups 4-stage, QA 4-stage) at desktop + mobile breakpoints, with empty-lane states, the lane-visibility affordance, and saved composed-view controls.

Density → Direction 3 is densest (everything visible); Direction 2 is most focused (one lane at a time); Direction 1 is in between (lanes scroll).
Standards → Directions 1 + 3 honor every existing rule. Direction 2 explicitly proposes amending the no-top-level-tab-bar rule to allow per-section sub-navigation.
